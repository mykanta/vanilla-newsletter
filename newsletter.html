IFF Opens Vanilla Innovation Centre in Madagascar to Drive Origin-Based R&D
International Flavors & Fragrances has inaugurated a 650-square-metre Vanilla Innovation Centre in Toamasina, Madagascar, consolidating lab analysis, extraction science, scent creation, and farmer collaboration under a single roof. The facility's proximity to vanilla-growing regions enables IFF to respond more rapidly to climate-related disruptions in the supply chain while deepening traceability programs with smallholder farming networks. The opening signals a broader industry shift toward investing in origin-based research rather than processing exclusively in consuming markets.

Madagascar Cyclones Trigger Export Delays and Supermarket Shortages
Severe cyclones battered Madagascar's eastern coast in early 2026, devastating vanilla orchid crops and halting curing and export operations across the island. Retailers across the United States began reporting shortages of pure vanilla extract as shipments stalled at origin, with prices at the shelf climbing sharply in response. Agricultural analysts warn the market could feel supply constraints for 18 to 24 months, as replanted vines require up to three years to bear harvestable fruit.
