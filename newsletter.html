IFF Opens Vanilla Innovation Center in the Heart of Madagascar
International Flavors & Fragrances has inaugurated a 650-square-meter Vanilla Innovation Center in Toamasina, Madagascar’s principal seaport, consolidating lab analysis, extraction science, and flavor creation under one roof in the world’s leading vanilla-growing region. The facility anchors IFF’s RE-MASTER VANILLA program, a structured initiative that trains local teams, hosts customer workshops, and strengthens sustainable sourcing practices from the ground up. By embedding R&D directly at origin, IFF is positioning itself to respond faster to crop variability while deepening traceability and quality assurance across its global vanilla supply chain.

Back-to-Back Cyclones Devastate Madagascar Crops, Sparking U.S. Shortages
A succession of severe cyclones tore through Madagascar’s northeastern growing corridors in early 2026, destroying wide swaths of the fragile vanilla orchid vines that supply roughly 80% of global demand and halting the curing and export pipeline entirely. American supermarkets have already begun reporting extract shortages, and analysts warn retail prices could double before the autumn baking season if replanting efforts are not accelerated. With new orchid vines requiring up to three years to bear commercial fruit, the market disruption is expected to persist well into 2027 or 2028.

Israeli Startup Vanilla Vida Achieves World’s First Large-Scale Indoor Vanilla Harvest
Israeli agtech startup Vanilla Vida has completed the world’s first commercially scaled harvest of vanilla grown entirely in a controlled indoor environment, cutting the traditional planting-to-bloom timeline roughly in half while producing beans with elevated vanillin concentrations. The breakthrough offers a potential buffer against the climate volatility that periodically devastates open-field crops in Madagascar and Indonesia, and comes at a critical moment as cyclone-driven supply shortages tighten global availability. Additional greenhouse facilities are ramping up production, with further harvests anticipated throughout the remainder of 2026.

Global Vanilla Extract Market Forecast to Hit $10.3 Billion by 2036
The global vanilla extract market, valued at approximately $5.89 billion in 2026, is projected to reach $10.30 billion by 2036 at a CAGR of 5.7%, fueled by a decisive consumer pivot toward natural, clean-label ingredients in food, beverage, and personal care categories. Liquid extract formats dominate with a 56% volume share, while North America leads regional demand at 39%, even as Asia-Pacific emerges as the fastest-growing segment. The structural shift away from synthetic vanillin continues to be the primary growth engine, with premium single-origin and organic variants outpacing the broader category.
