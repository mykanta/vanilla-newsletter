IFF Opens Vanilla Innovation Centre in Madagascar to Future-Proof Global Supply
International Flavors & Fragrances inaugurated a 650-square-metre Vanilla Innovation Centre in Toamasina, Madagascar’s principal seaport, bringing lab analysis, extraction, and flavour application development under one roof for the first time at origin. The facility is designed to craft region-specific flavour profiles while strengthening long-term resilience in a supply chain that accounts for roughly 80 percent of the world’s vanilla output. IFF executives stated the centre underscores vanilla’s designation as a strategic priority tonality across the company’s flavour and fragrance divisions.

Back-to-Back Cyclones Halt Madagascar Exports, Sending Extract Prices to Multi-Year Highs
Unprecedented successive cyclones devastated Madagascar’s vanilla-growing regions in early 2026, halting bean exports and triggering supply alarms across the global flavours industry. Industry analysts warn that shortages could persist for 18 to 24 months, as farmers must replant vines that require up to three years to bear fruit. American supermarkets are already reporting vanilla extract shortages, and wholesale extract prices have climbed to their highest levels in several years.

Vanilla Vida Celebrates World’s First Large-Scale Indoor Vanilla Harvest
Israeli agri-tech startup Vanilla Vida has announced the world’s first commercial-scale harvest of vanilla grown entirely indoors, using a controlled-environment system that halves the planting-to-bloom timeline compared to traditional field cultivation. The company reports higher vanillin concentrations in its pods than typical outdoor-grown beans, positioning the product for premium food and fragrance markets. Additional greenhouse sites are already producing crops slated for further harvests through the remainder of 2026, signalling a potential paradigm shift in global vanilla production.

Vanilla Extract Market Forecast to Reach USD 10.3 Billion by 2036, Driven by Clean-Label Demand
A new industry report projects the global vanilla extract market will expand from USD 5.89 billion in 2026 to USD 10.3 billion by 2036, a compound annual growth rate of 5.7 percent. The primary driver is a structural consumer shift away from synthetic vanillin toward authentic natural extracts, with food and beverage applications capturing the largest segment at 43.7 percent of market share. North America holds a 39.2 percent regional share, though Asia-Pacific is forecast to register the fastest growth over the decade ahead.

North America Commands 37.8% of Global Vanilla Bean Market as Demand Heads Toward $2.3B
North America has consolidated its position as the world’s largest vanilla bean demand centre, holding a 37.8 percent share of the global market, according to new data from Mordor Intelligence. Analysts attribute the region’s dominance to deep-rooted usage in baked goods, ice cream, and premium beverage categories, compounded by a clean-label trend favouring natural over artificial flavourings. The global vanilla bean sub-segment is independently projected to reach USD 2.3 billion by 2031 as emerging markets adopt vanilla-forward product formulations.

U.S. Researchers Race to Establish Domestic Vanilla Cultivation in Florida & Puerto Rico
University of Florida researchers and SARE grant programmes are actively developing viable pathways for commercial vanilla cultivation in southern Florida, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands, seeking to reduce American dependence on tropical imports. Vanilla’s high potential market value—estimated at up to USD 150,000 per acre—makes it one of the most lucrative cash crop alternatives for subtropical growers willing to invest in the multi-year cultivation cycle. Domestically grown vanilla could shorten supply chains and improve traceability for brands prioritising transparent, clean-label sourcing strategies.
